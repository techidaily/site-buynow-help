##  What to Look For in an iPhone 15 Pro Max Case in 2023

 The first thing you need to do is forget the idea of using the case for your iPhone 14 Pro Max, assuming you have one. While the new phone looks similar, it’s a different size with a different shape to the curves on the edge of the phone, so the older case won’t fit properly.

 For a new case, you need to choose which type of form factor you’re looking at, and how much grip you need. The iPhone 15 Pro Max isn’t the largest phone on the market, but as the biggest iPhone, it’s the easiest to drop, so a case with extra grip is useful. Aside from this, you can pick from anywhere on the spectrum from ultra-thin and light to larger and bulkier.

 If you’re prone to dropping your phone, or you’re often using it in environments that put it in significant danger, you’ll want to opt for a rugged case. These are bulkier but offer much more protection. If you don’t want to go this far, folio-style designs protect your screen and sometimes double as a wallet.

 Next, you’ll want to consider the material you want to use. Many cases use [thermoplastic polyurethane](https://win-blog.techidaily.com/troubleshoot-steamvr-issues-in-minutes-with-this-simple-guide/) (TPU) or plastic composite, which are relatively affordable but offer great protection. Of course, you can also choose more premium materials like leather or micro twill, which Apple uses in its own FineWoven cases.

 These materials can add to the price, so you need to decide how much you want to spend. Fortunately, this doesn’t have to affect how protected your phone is, as even budget cases can offer excellent protection. Aside from premium materials, spending more also gets you features like [MagSafe](https://techno-recovery.techidaily.com/mastering-regional-adjustments-how-to-update-regions-on-your-iphone/).

 Finally, don’t forget to have fun with the look. Regardless of material or price point, many cases come in various fun color options, but you can also choose a clear case to see your iPhone’s glorious finish better underneath.
